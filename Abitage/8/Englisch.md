**_Excerise 1:_** **Read** the text and **decide** if the statements are true or false.


<h3>Our Town, Our Future</h3>

In November, Class 8b started a media project called “Our Town, Our Future”. The idea was simple: each group had to choose one local problem, research it carefully and present realistic solutions in English. Some students wrote about traffic near the school, others examined the lack of green spaces, and one group focused on the old swimming pool, which had been closed for years.

Nora’s group chose the swimming pool because many younger children in the town had no safe place to learn how to swim. At first, the students assumed that the building was beyond repair. However, after they had spoken to a local architect, they realised that part of the structure could still be used. The group then interviewed parents, teachers and sports clubs. They also carried out an online survey and received more than 200 answers within four days.

While Nora was analysing the results, Leon and Fatima were preparing a short video for the final presentation. They compared old photographs with recent ones and added comments in English. The group wanted to sound convincing, so they practised several times and improved their pronunciation after getting feedback from their teacher.

On presentation day, the classroom looked more like a conference room than a normal school lesson. The audience asked critical questions, but Nora’s group answered them calmly and with clear arguments. In the end, their idea was not to rebuild the entire pool immediately, but to reopen one part of it step by step. Even the head teacher said that their proposal sounded realistic and well researched.
